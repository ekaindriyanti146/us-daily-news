## Introduction
The **National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA)** has successfully fueled up the giant **Artemis 2** moon rocket, despite encountering some leaks during the process. The rocket, which is scheduled to launch soon, is a crucial part of the **Artemis program**, a mission aimed at returning humans to the moon by 2025. The Artemis 2 mission will send astronauts on a lunar flyby, testing the capabilities of the **Space Launch System (SLS)** rocket and the **Orion spacecraft**. This mission is a significant step towards establishing a sustainable presence on the lunar surface and eventually sending humans to Mars.

## Background & Context
The **Artemis program** was announced in 2019, with the goal of returning humans to the moon by 2025. The program involves several missions, including the **Artemis 1** uncrewed mission, which launched in November 2022, and the **Artemis 2** crewed mission, which is scheduled to launch soon. The **Artemis 2** mission will send astronauts on a lunar flyby, testing the capabilities of the **SLS** rocket and the **Orion spacecraft**. The success of the Artemis 2 mission is crucial for the future of space exploration, including plans for a manned mission to Mars.

The **SLS** rocket is the most powerful rocket ever built, with the capability to carry heavy payloads to the moon and beyond. The **Orion spacecraft** is designed to carry astronauts to the moon and provide a safe and reliable means of transportation. The **Artemis 2** mission will test the capabilities of both the **SLS** rocket and the **Orion spacecraft**, paving the way for future missions to the moon and beyond.
