The Workers' Party of Korea (WPK) has been the ruling party of North Korea since its founding in 1948. The party congress is the highest decision-making body of the WPK, and it is responsible for setting the country's overall direction and policy. The congress is typically held every five years, but it can be convened at any time by the party's central committee. The last party congress was held in 2021, and it was marked by significant changes to the party's leadership and policy. This year's congress is expected to be just as significant, with many analysts predicting that Kim Jong-un will use the occasion to announce major policy initiatives and consolidate his power.

The party congress has a long history in North Korea, dating back to the early days of the WPK. The first party congress was held in 1948, and it was attended by Kim Il-sung, the founder of the WPK.
