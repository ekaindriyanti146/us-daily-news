## Introduction
In a stark warning, **Germany's Chancellor Friedrich Merz** has declared that the world's rules-based order 'no longer exists'. This statement, made at the **Munich Security Conference**, highlights the growing concerns among global leaders about the erosion of international norms and the rise of **nationalism** and **protectionism**. As the world grapples with the implications of this shift, **Merz**'s warning serves as a call to action for **European leaders** to come together and address the crisis. The **US**, **China**, and **Russia** are all major players in this new global landscape, and their actions will have far-reaching consequences.

## Background & Context
The rules-based order, which has underpinned global relations since the end of **World War II**, is facing unprecedented challenges. The rise of **China** as a global power, the **US**'s increasing unilateralism, and **Russia**'s aggressive behavior have all contributed to the erosion of this order. The **COVID-19 pandemic** has further accelerated this trend, as countries have turned inward to address their own domestic crises. The consequences of this collapse are far-reaching, with implications for global trade, security, and human rights. As **Merz** noted, the world is now facing a 'wrecking ball' of **nationalism** and **protectionism**, which threatens to destroy the very foundations of international cooperation.

### Historical Context
The rules-based order was established in the aftermath of **World War II**, with the aim of promoting peace, stability, and cooperation among nations. The **United Nations**, the **European Union**, and other international institutions were created to facilitate this cooperation and promote a set of common values and norms. However, in recent years, these institutions have faced significant challenges, including the rise of **populism** and **nationalism**, which have eroded trust in international cooperation.

The collapse of the rules-based order has significant implications for **US citizens**. As the **US** is a major player in global affairs, its citizens will be affected by the rise of **nationalism** and **protectionism**. As **Merz** warned, the world is now facing a 'perfect storm' of challenges, which requires a unified and coordinated response from **European leaders**.

### Economic Implications
The collapse of the rules-based order will have significant economic implications, including the rise of **protectionism** and **trade wars**. The **US**'s withdrawal from international trade agreements, such as the **Trans-Pacific Partnership**, has already had significant consequences for global trade. The **US-China trade war** has also had far-reaching implications, with significant tariffs imposed on both sides. The erosion of international norms and institutions will further exacerbate these trends, leading to increased uncertainty and risk for businesses and investors.
