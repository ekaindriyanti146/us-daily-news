They have been used for decades to treat bacterial infections, particularly in Eastern Europe and the former Soviet Union. However, the use of phage therapy has been limited by the lack of standardized methods for isolating and characterizing bacteriophages. The **Golden Gate method** changes this, allowing scientists to engineer bacteriophages from scratch using a combination of genetic engineering and synthetic biology techniques.
