## Background & Context
Measles is a highly contagious and potentially serious disease that can cause fever, rash, and respiratory symptoms. It is spread through the air when an infected person coughs or sneezes, and can also be spread through contact with contaminated surfaces. The disease was once nearly eradicated in the United States, but has seen a resurgence in recent years due to low vaccination rates and the spread of misinformation about vaccine safety. According to the **Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC)**, measles cases have increased significantly in recent years, with over 1,000 cases reported in 2022.
