## Background & Context
The Nipah virus is a **zoonotic virus**, meaning it can be transmitted between animals and humans. The virus is typically found in **bats** and can be transmitted to humans through **direct contact with infected animals or people**. The virus was first identified in 1998 in Malaysia and has since been reported in several countries, including India, Bangladesh, and Cambodia. The Nipah virus is considered a **highly infectious and deadly disease**, with a mortality rate of up to 90% in some cases.

### What is the Nipah Virus?
The Nipah virus is a member of the **Paramyxoviridae family**, which includes other viruses such as measles and mumps. The virus is typically spread through **respiratory droplets** or **contact with infected animals**. The symptoms of the Nipah virus include **fever, headache, and respiratory problems**, which can progress to more severe symptoms such as **encephalitis** (inflammation of the brain) and **septicemia** (blood poisoning).
