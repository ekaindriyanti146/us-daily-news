A recent **Russian** strike on **Ukraine**'s capital city, **Kyiv**, has left a significant portion of the city without heat, according to **Kyiv**'s mayor. The attack, which is part of a larger campaign by **Russia** to target **Ukraine**'s critical infrastructure, has raised concerns about the humanitarian situation in **Ukraine**, particularly during the cold winter months. As the conflict between **Ukraine** and **Russia** continues, the international community has condemned **Russia**'s actions and called for an end to the violence.

## Background & Context
The conflict between **Ukraine** and **Russia** began in 2014, when **Russia** annexed the **Crimean Peninsula** from **Ukraine**. Since then, **Russia** has supported separatist groups in eastern **Ukraine**, leading to a protracted conflict that has resulted in thousands of deaths and displaced millions of people. In recent months, **Russia** has escalated its attacks on **Ukraine**, targeting critical infrastructure, including energy facilities, in an effort to weaken the country's ability to resist **Russian** aggression.

The latest strike on **Kyiv** is part of this campaign, and has left 1,330 buildings without heat, according to the city's mayor. The attack has also damaged other critical infrastructure, including power plants and water treatment facilities. The **Ukrainian** government has accused **Russia** of deliberately targeting civilians and critical infrastructure, in an effort to intimidate the population and undermine the country's ability to function.
