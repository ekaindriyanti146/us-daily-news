### Understanding the Science
Researchers have long known that mentally stimulating activities can have a positive impact on cognitive function. Studies have shown that engaging in activities that challenge the brain, such as learning a new language or playing a musical instrument, can build cognitive reserve and reduce the risk of dementia. The latest study takes this research a step further, suggesting that even simple, everyday tasks can have a significant impact on delaying Alzheimer's and dementia. But how does this work? According to scientists, mentally stimulating activities can help build cognitive reserve by promoting the growth of new neurons and forming new connections between brain cells. This can help to compensate for age-related cognitive decline and reduce the risk of dementia.
