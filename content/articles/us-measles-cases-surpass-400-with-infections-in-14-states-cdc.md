The disease was once nearly eradicated in the US, thanks to widespread vaccination efforts. However, in recent years, there has been a decline in vaccination rates, particularly among children. This decline has been attributed to misinformation and misconceptions about the safety and effectiveness of vaccines. The current outbreak is a result of this decline in vaccination rates, with many of the reported cases being linked to unvaccinated individuals.

Health officials are concerned about the potential loss of measles elimination status in the US, which would have significant implications for public health.
