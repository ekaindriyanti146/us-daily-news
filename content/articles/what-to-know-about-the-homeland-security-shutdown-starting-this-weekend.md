The **Homeland Security** shutdown is the result of a **funding dispute** between **Congress** and the **White House**. The **Department of Homeland Security** (DHS) is responsible for a wide range of critical **functions**, including **border security**, **immigration enforcement**, and **disaster response**. However, the agency has been operating on **temporary funding** for several months, and a **long-term funding agreement** has yet to be reached. The shutdown will affect various **government agencies**, including **US Customs and Border Protection**, **US Immigration and Customs Enforcement**, and the **Federal Emergency Management Agency** (FEMA).

### History of the Shutdown
The **Homeland Security** shutdown is not the first of its kind. In 2019, the **US government** experienced a **35-day shutdown**, which was the longest in **US history**. The shutdown had significant **consequences**, including **delayed pay** for **federal workers** and **disruptions** to **critical services**. This time around, the shutdown is expected to have even more **far-reaching impacts**, given the critical **role** that **Homeland Security** plays in **national security** and **immigration**.
