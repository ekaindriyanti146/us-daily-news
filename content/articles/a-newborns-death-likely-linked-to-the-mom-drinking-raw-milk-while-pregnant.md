* Pregnant women are advised to avoid consuming raw milk and raw milk products due to the risk of infection.

## Introduction
A recent incident in New Mexico has highlighted the dangers of consuming raw milk during pregnancy. A newborn's death has been linked to the mother's consumption of raw milk, which was contaminated with **listeria**. This incident has raised concerns about the safety of raw milk and the risks associated with its consumption, particularly for pregnant women. According to reports, the mother had consumed raw milk while pregnant, which led to the infection being passed on to the infant. The **New Mexico Department of Health** has issued a warning against consuming raw milk, citing the risk of **listeria** and other bacterial infections.

## Background & Context
Raw milk is milk that has not been pasteurized, a process that kills bacteria and other microorganisms. While some people believe that raw milk has health benefits, it can also pose a significant risk of infection, particularly for vulnerable populations such as pregnant women and young children. **Listeria** is a type of bacteria that can be found in raw milk and can cause serious illness, including miscarriage, stillbirth, and infant death. The **Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC)** estimates that approximately 1,600 people in the United States are infected with **listeria** each year, resulting in approximately 260 deaths.

### The Risks of Raw Milk
The consumption of raw milk is a significant risk factor for **listeria** infection. According to the **CDC**, raw milk is 150 times more likely to cause **listeria** infection than pasteurized milk. Pregnant women are particularly vulnerable to **listeria** infection, as their immune systems are weakened during pregnancy. The **American College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists (ACOG)** recommends that pregnant women avoid consuming raw milk and raw milk products due to the risk of infection.

## Analysis
The incident in New Mexico highlights the importance of safe food handling practices, particularly for pregnant women. The consumption of raw milk is a significant risk factor for **listeria** infection, and pregnant women should be aware of the risks associated with its consumption. The **FDA** has announced new testing of baby formula to ensure safety, which is a positive step towards preventing similar incidents in the future. However, more needs to be done to raise awareness about the risks associated with raw milk consumption, particularly among pregnant women.

### The Importance of Pasteurization
Pasteurization is a critical step in ensuring the safety of milk and other dairy products. The process involves heating the milk to a high temperature to kill bacteria and other microorganisms. **Pasteurization** is a simple and effective way to prevent the spread of infection, and it is essential for protecting public health. The **World Health Organization (WHO)** recommends that all milk and dairy products be pasteurized to prevent the spread of infection.
