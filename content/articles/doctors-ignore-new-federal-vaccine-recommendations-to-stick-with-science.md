## Introduction
In a surprising move, many US doctors are choosing to ignore new federal vaccine recommendations, citing concerns over the lack of scientific evidence and potential risks to patient safety. The decision has sparked a heated debate among medical professionals, with some arguing that the recommendations are not based on the best available science. As the situation continues to unfold, it is clear that the issue of vaccine recommendations has become a contentious one, with far-reaching implications for public health and vaccine policy in the US. The **Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC)**, a key player in the development of vaccine recommendations, has faced criticism from some medical professionals who argue that the new guidelines are not supported by sufficient evidence.

## Background & Context
The controversy surrounding vaccine recommendations in the US is not new. In recent years, there have been numerous debates and discussions about the safety and efficacy of various vaccines, with some arguing that the current vaccine schedule is too aggressive and may be putting children at risk. The **American Academy of Pediatrics (AAP)**, a leading professional organization for pediatricians, has been at the forefront of these discussions, advocating for a more nuanced approach to vaccine recommendations that takes into account the unique needs and circumstances of individual children. The **CDC** has also been working to address concerns and provide more detailed guidance on vaccine administration, including the development of new recommendations for childhood vaccines. However, the latest recommendations have been met with skepticism by some doctors, who argue that they are not supported by sufficient scientific evidence.

## Analysis
The decision by US doctors to defy new federal vaccine recommendations is a significant development that highlights the complexities and challenges of vaccine policy in the US. On one hand, the move reflects a commitment to patient safety and a desire to ensure that vaccine recommendations are based on the best available science. On the other hand, it also raises concerns about the potential for inconsistent and confusing vaccine guidance, which could undermine public trust in the vaccine system and put vulnerable populations at risk. As **Dr. Anthony Fauci**, a leading expert on infectious diseases, has noted, "Vaccines are a critical tool in the prevention of infectious diseases, and it is essential that we have a robust and evidence-based approach to vaccine recommendations." The **National Institutes of Health (NIH)** has also emphasized the importance of evidence-based medicine in guiding vaccine policy.

## Quotes & Reactions
"We are concerned that the new recommendations are not supported by sufficient scientific evidence, and we cannot in good conscience recommend them to our patients," said **Dr. Jennifer Dyer**, a pediatrician and spokesperson for the **American Academy of Pediatrics**. "As doctors, we have a responsibility to our patients to provide them with the best possible care, and that includes ensuring that vaccine recommendations are based on the latest and most reliable scientific evidence." **Dr. Peter Hotez**, a leading expert on vaccine development, has also weighed in on the issue, noting that "The science on vaccines is clear: they are safe and effective, and they have been proven to save countless lives.
