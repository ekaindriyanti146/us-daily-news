## Introduction
On January 20, 2024, the **DC Health** department issued a warning about potential measles exposure at the March for Life event, which took place on January 19, 2024. The warning comes after multiple cases of measles were confirmed in DC, with several public places identified as possible infection sites. The **March for Life** event, which is an annual anti-abortion rally, drew thousands of people to the National Mall in Washington, D.C. The warning is a reminder that **measles** is a highly contagious disease that can be spread through the air when an infected person coughs or sneezes.

## Background & Context
Measles is a viral disease that can cause fever, cough, runny nose, and a rash. It is highly contagious and can be spread through the air when an infected person coughs or sneezes. The **measles vaccine** is highly effective in preventing the disease, but it is not 100% effective. According to the **Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC)**, two doses of the measles vaccine are about 97% effective in preventing measles. However, people who are not vaccinated are at a higher risk of infection. The **DC Health** department is warning people who attended the March for Life event to be aware of the symptoms of measles and to seek medical attention if they experience any of them.

## Analysis
The potential measles exposure at the March for Life event is a concern for **public health officials**. Measles is a highly contagious disease that can spread quickly in crowded areas. The **DC Health** department is working to identify people who may have been exposed to the virus and to provide them with information about how to prevent the spread of the disease.
