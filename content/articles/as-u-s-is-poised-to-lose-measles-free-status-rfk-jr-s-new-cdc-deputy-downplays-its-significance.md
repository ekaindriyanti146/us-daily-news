## Key Takeaways
* The US is poised to lose its measles-free status due to a surge in cases
* RFK Jr.'s new CDC deputy downplays the significance of losing measles-free status
* Measles cases have been increasing in recent years, with a record-breaking year in 2022

## Introduction
The United States is on the brink of losing its measles-free status, a designation it has held since 2000. This comes as a result of a surge in measles cases across the country, with many outbreaks reported in recent years. The news has sparked concern among public health officials, who warn that the loss of measles-free status could have significant implications for the country's health and wellbeing. According to reports, RFK Jr.'s new CDC deputy has downplayed the significance of losing measles-free status, calling it a "cost of doing business."

## Background & Context
Measles is a highly contagious and potentially deadly disease that was once thought to be eradicated in the US. However, in recent years, there has been a resurgence of cases, largely due to low vaccination rates in certain communities. The [CDC](https://www.cdc.gov/) has reported a record-breaking number of measles cases in 2022, with over 1,000 cases reported nationwide. This surge in cases has been attributed to a combination of factors, including misinformation about vaccine safety and low vaccination rates in certain communities.

The US has been considered measles-free since 2000, when the [CDC](https://www.cdc.gov/) declared that the disease had been eliminated. However, this designation is now at risk due to the surge in cases. The [World Health Organization](https://www.who.int/) defines measles elimination as the absence of continuous transmission of the disease for at least 12 months. The US has been struggling to meet this criteria, with ongoing outbreaks reported in several states.

## Analysis
The loss of measles-free status could have significant implications for public health in the US. Measles is a highly contagious disease that can spread quickly in communities with low vaccination rates. If the US loses its measles-free status, it could lead to a surge in cases, potentially resulting in serious illness and even death. Additionally, the loss of measles-free status could also have economic implications, as it could lead to increased healthcare costs and lost productivity.

The downplaying of the significance of losing measles-free status by RFK Jr.'s new CDC deputy has sparked concern among public health officials. Many experts argue that the loss of measles-free status is a serious public health concern that requires immediate attention. "Losing measles-free status is not just a matter of semantics, it's a reflection of our inability to protect our communities from a preventable disease," said Dr. [Peter Hotez](https://www.bcm.edu/people-search/peter-hotez-12640), a leading expert on vaccine-preventable diseases.

## Quotes & Reactions
"The loss of measles-free status is a wake-up call for our country," said Dr. [Nancy Messonnier](https://www.cdc.gov/about/leadership/leaders/nancy-messonier.htm), director of the [CDC](https://www.cdc.gov/)'s National Center for Immunization and Respiratory Diseases. "We need to take immediate action to protect our communities from this preventable disease."

RFK Jr.'s new CDC deputy, however, has downplayed the significance of losing measles-free status. "Losing measles-free status is a cost of doing business," said the deputy. "We need to focus on the bigger picture and not get bogged down in semantics."
