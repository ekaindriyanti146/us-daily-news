## Introduction
On Valentine's Day, a team of astronauts from SpaceX's Crew-12 mission arrived at the International Space Station (ISS), marking a significant milestone in the partnership between SpaceX and **NASA**. The crew, consisting of four astronauts, docked at the ISS in the morning, bringing much-needed relief to the skeleton crew that had been manning the station. The successful docking is a testament to the capabilities of SpaceX's Dragon spacecraft and the expertise of the astronauts on board. In this article, we will delve into the details of the mission, the significance of the docking, and what this means for the future of space exploration.

## Background & Context
The ISS has been continuously occupied by astronauts since 2000, with crews rotating every six months. However, due to various reasons, including the evacuation of **NASA**'s crew, the ISS had been operating with a skeleton crew. The arrival of the SpaceX Crew-12 mission is a welcome boost to the station, allowing for a full crew to conduct various scientific experiments and maintenance tasks. The Crew-12 mission is part of **SpaceX**'s ongoing partnership with **NASA**, which aims to provide reliable and efficient transportation services to the ISS.

### The SpaceX Crew-12 Mission
The SpaceX Crew-12 mission launched on Friday, February 13th, from Kennedy Space Center's Launch Complex 39A. The crew, consisting of astronauts from **NASA** and international partners, traveled to the ISS on board the Dragon spacecraft. The journey took approximately 24 hours, during which the crew performed various checks and preparations for the docking. The successful launch and docking of the Crew-12 mission mark a significant milestone in the development of commercial crew vehicles.
