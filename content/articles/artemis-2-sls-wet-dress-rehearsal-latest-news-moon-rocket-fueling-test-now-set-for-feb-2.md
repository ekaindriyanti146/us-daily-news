## Introduction
The **National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA)** has announced that the **Artemis 2** mission, which aims to send astronauts back to the **Moon** for the first time in half a century, is set to launch in February. The mission will use the **Space Launch System (SLS)** rocket, which is currently undergoing a wet dress rehearsal, a crucial test of the rocket's systems before launch. The wet dress rehearsal, which was previously scheduled for January, has been rescheduled for February 2 due to extreme cold weather at the launch site. In this article, we will delve into the details of the **Artemis 2** mission, its objectives, and the significance of the wet dress rehearsal.

## Background & Context
The **Artemis 2** mission is part of NASA's **Artemis program**, which aims to establish a sustainable human presence on the **Moon** by 2025. The program is a major step towards sending humans to **Mars** and beyond, with the **Moon** serving as a stepping stone for deeper space exploration. The **Artemis 2** mission will send a crew of four astronauts on a lunar flyby, testing the **SLS** rocket and the **Orion** spacecraft, which will carry the astronauts to the **Moon**. The mission will also test the spacecraft's systems, including its propulsion, communication, and life support systems.

The **SLS** rocket is a heavy-lift rocket that is capable of carrying heavy payloads, including the **Orion** spacecraft, to the **Moon** and beyond. The rocket is currently undergoing testing at NASA's **Kennedy Space Center**, where the wet dress rehearsal is taking place. The rehearsal involves fueling the rocket with liquid hydrogen and liquid oxygen, and then draining the fuel tanks, to test the rocket's systems and ensure that they are functioning properly.
