## Introduction
NASA has restarted the countdown for the Artemis II mission, a critical step towards sending humans back to the Moon. The mission was delayed due to hydrogen leaks on the Space Launch System (SLS) rocket, but after repairs, the countdown has resumed. The **Artemis II** mission aims to send the first woman and the first person of color to the Moon by 2025, marking a significant milestone in space exploration. The mission will test the capabilities of the SLS rocket and the **Orion** spacecraft, which will carry the astronauts to the Moon. The success of the Artemis II mission is crucial for the future of space exploration, including the planned **Artemis III** mission, which will send astronauts to the lunar surface.

## Background & Context
The **Artemis II** mission is part of NASA's larger Artemis program, which aims to establish a sustainable human presence on the Moon by 2028. The program involves several missions, including the **Artemis I** mission, which was an uncrewed test flight of the SLS rocket and the Orion spacecraft. The **Artemis II** mission will be the first crewed mission, with four astronauts on board, including the first woman and the first person of color to travel to the Moon. The mission will last for approximately 26 days, during which the astronauts will conduct scientific experiments and test the capabilities of the SLS rocket and the Orion spacecraft.

The SLS rocket is the most powerful rocket ever built, with a payload capacity of over 270,000 pounds. The rocket is designed to carry heavy payloads, including the Orion spacecraft, to the Moon and beyond. The **Orion** spacecraft is designed to carry astronauts to the Moon and provide life support systems for the crew during the mission. The spacecraft is equipped with advanced technology, including a state-of-the-art navigation system and a robust communication system.
