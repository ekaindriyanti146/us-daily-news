* The UK has expanded its visa scheme to allow more **Hong Kong** residents to live and work in the UK, citing concerns over the erosion of freedoms in the territory.

## Introduction
In a significant escalation of tensions between the UK and China, Beijing has lashed out at London's decision to expand its visa scheme for **Hong Kong** residents. The move comes in response to the conviction of **Jimmy Lai**, a pro-democracy tycoon who has been sentenced to 20 years in prison under **Hong Kong**'s national security law. The UK has cited concerns over the erosion of freedoms in the territory as the reason for expanding its visa scheme, which will allow more **Hong Kong** residents to live and work in the UK. The decision has sparked a diplomatic row between the two countries, with China accusing the UK of interfering in its internal affairs.

## Background & Context
**Jimmy Lai**'s conviction is the latest in a series of moves by the Chinese government to crack down on dissent in **Hong Kong**. The territory has been rocked by pro-democracy protests in recent years, which have been met with increasingly harsh responses from the authorities. The introduction of the national security law has given the government sweeping powers to suppress opposition, and **Jimmy Lai**'s conviction is seen as a test case for the law. The UK has been a vocal critic of China's actions in **Hong Kong**, and the expansion of its visa scheme is seen as a way of providing a safe haven for those who are fleeing persecution.

The UK's visa scheme, known as the **British National (Overseas) visa**, was introduced in January 2021 and allows **Hong Kong** residents to live and work in the UK for up to five years. The scheme has been popular, with thousands of applications already received. However, the UK has now expanded the scheme to allow more **Hong Kong** residents to apply, citing concerns over the erosion of freedoms in the territory.

## Quotes & Reactions
The reaction to the UK's decision has been swift and fierce. **Chinese Foreign Ministry spokesperson** Zhao Lijian accused the UK of interfering in China's internal affairs, saying: "The UK's move is a blatant interference in China's internal affairs and a gross violation of China's sovereignty." He added: "We strongly condemn this and urge the UK to stop its wrongdoing."

In contrast, **UK Foreign Secretary** Liz Truss welcomed the expansion of the visa scheme, saying: "The UK is committed to providing a safe haven for those who are fleeing persecution in **Hong Kong**. We will continue to stand up for democratic values and protect those who are persecuted for their beliefs."
