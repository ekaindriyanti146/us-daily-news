## Introduction
French tech giant **Capgemini** has announced that it will sell its US subsidiary, which has been working with **US Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE)** to track immigrants. The decision comes after the company faced criticism from human rights groups and lawmakers over its involvement with **ICE**. **Capgemini** has stated that the sale is part of its strategy to focus on its core business and to address the controversy surrounding its contract with **ICE**. The company has not disclosed the terms of the sale or the buyer.

## Background & Context
**Capgemini**'s US subsidiary has been providing technology services to **ICE** since 2018, including data analytics and software development. The contract has been valued at over $100 million. However, the company has faced criticism from human rights groups and lawmakers over its involvement with **ICE**, which has been accused of human rights abuses and separating families at the US-Mexico border. French MPs have also demanded an explanation over the company's contract with **ICE**, citing concerns over the treatment of migrants and the company's reputation.

Despite the criticism, **Capgemini** had initially defended its contract with **ICE**, stating that it was providing technology services to help the agency with its mission to enforce immigration laws.
