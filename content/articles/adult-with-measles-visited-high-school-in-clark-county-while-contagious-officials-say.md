## Introduction
A recent incident in Clark County has raised concerns about a potential measles outbreak. According to health officials, an adult with measles visited a high school in the area while contagious, putting students and staff at risk of infection. The news has sparked warnings from health authorities, who are advising individuals to take precautions to prevent the spread of the disease. In this article, we will delve into the details of the incident, the risks associated with measles, and the measures being taken to prevent an outbreak.

## Background & Context
Measles is a highly contagious virus that can cause serious health complications, particularly in young children and individuals with weakened immune systems. The virus is spread through the air when an infected person coughs or sneezes, and can also be transmitted through contact with contaminated surfaces. Symptoms of measles include fever, cough, runny nose, and a characteristic rash. In severe cases, measles can lead to complications such as pneumonia, encephalitis, and even death.

The incident in Clark County is not an isolated event. According to the [CDC](https://www.cdc.gov/measles/index.html), there have been several measles outbreaks in the United States in recent years, often linked to international travel or unvaccinated individuals. The [measles outbreak](/articles/us-measles-cases-surpass-400-with-infections-in-14-states-cdc) in 2019, for example, resulted in over 1,200 cases of measles across 31 states.
