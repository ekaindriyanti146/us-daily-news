## Introduction
Colorectal cancer, also known as colon cancer, has become the leading cause of cancer death in people under 50 in the US, according to recent reports. This alarming trend has sparked concern among healthcare professionals and researchers, who are working to understand the underlying causes of this increase. In this article, we will delve into the background and context of this issue, analyze the reasons behind the rise in colorectal cancer deaths, and explore the implications for young adults and the healthcare system.

## Background & Context
Colorectal cancer is a type of cancer that affects the colon or rectum, and it is typically associated with older adults. However, in recent years, there has been a significant increase in the number of young adults diagnosed with colorectal cancer. According to the [American Cancer Society](https://www.cancer.org/), the death rate from colorectal cancer in people under 50 has increased by 22% between 2012 and 2016. This trend is particularly concerning, as it suggests that colorectal cancer is becoming a major health threat for young adults.

The exact cause of the increase in colorectal cancer deaths is unknown, but several factors may contribute to this trend. These include:
* **Diet and lifestyle**: A diet high in processed meat, sugar, and saturated fat, combined with a sedentary lifestyle, may increase the risk of colorectal cancer.
* **Genetic predisposition**: Some people may be more susceptible to colorectal cancer due to their genetic makeup.
* **Lack of screening**: Colorectal cancer is often asymptomatic in its early stages, and many young adults may not undergo regular screening tests, such as colonoscopies.

## Analysis
The rise in colorectal cancer deaths among young adults has significant implications for the healthcare system and for individuals. Colorectal cancer is a preventable disease, and early detection and screening can greatly improve treatment outcomes. However, many young adults may not be aware of the risks and symptoms of colorectal cancer, and may not prioritize screening tests.

Furthermore, the increase in colorectal cancer deaths among young adults may be related to broader societal trends, such as the increasing prevalence of obesity and sedentary lifestyles. As the [US Daily](https://www.usdaily.com/) has reported, the [obesity epidemic](https://www.usdaily.com/articles/obesity-epidemic-in-the-us) is a major public health concern, and it may be contributing to the rise in colorectal cancer deaths.
