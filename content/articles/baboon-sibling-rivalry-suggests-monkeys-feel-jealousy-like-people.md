### Evolution of Sibling Rivalry
The evolution of sibling rivalry in baboons is thought to be an adaptation to the challenges of growing up in a competitive social environment. In baboon troops, resources such as food, water, and mating opportunities are limited, leading to intense competition among individuals. Sibling rivalry is a way for young baboons to establish their dominance and secure access to these resources, increasing their chances of survival and reproduction. This behavior is also seen in other primates, such as chimpanzees, and is thought to be an evolutionary precursor to human sibling rivalry.
