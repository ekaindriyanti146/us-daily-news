## Background & Context
The vaccine trial in question was intended to test the efficacy of a hepatitis B vaccine in babies in Guinea-Bissau. The trial was funded by the **US government** and was scheduled to take place in the West African country. However, the trial was met with criticism from local health officials and international organizations, who raised concerns about the ethics of the trial and the potential risks to the subjects.

The **Tuskegee experiment**, which took place in the United States from 1932 to 1972, was a notorious example of unethical medical research. In the experiment, hundreds of African American men were left untreated for syphilis, despite the availability of effective treatments, in order to study the progression of the disease. The experiment was widely condemned and led to significant changes in the way that medical research is conducted and regulated.
