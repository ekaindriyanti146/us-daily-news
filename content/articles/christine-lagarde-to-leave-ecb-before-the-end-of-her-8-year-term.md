As the president of the ECB, Lagarde has played a crucial role in shaping the bank's policies and responding to economic challenges. Her departure is likely to have significant implications for the European economy and monetary policy.

## Background & Context
Lagarde took over as president of the ECB in 2019, succeeding **Mario Draghi**. During her tenure, she has overseen a period of significant economic change, including the **COVID-19 pandemic** and the resulting economic downturn. Under her leadership, the ECB has implemented a range of policies aimed at supporting the economy, including **quantitative easing** and **negative interest rates**.

Despite these challenges, the ECB has maintained its commitment to price stability and has taken steps to support the economy. However, the bank's policies have not been without controversy, with some critics arguing that they have contributed to **inequality** and **inflation**.

The news of Lagarde's departure has sparked speculation about who might succeed her as president of the ECB. The **EU Governments** may want to fast-track the succession process to ensure a smooth transition and maintain stability in the European economy.
