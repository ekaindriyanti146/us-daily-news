The Taipei 101 skyscraper, formerly known as the Taipei World Financial Center, is a 101-floor building that stands at a height of 508 meters (1,667 feet). Completed in 2004, it was the world's tallest building until 2010, when it was surpassed by the Burj Khalifa in Dubai. The building's unique design and challenging architecture make it an attractive destination for thrill-seekers and adventure enthusiasts.

Honnold's climb was not without its challenges. The weather conditions in Taipei were unfavorable, with strong winds and heavy rain threatening to postpone the climb.
