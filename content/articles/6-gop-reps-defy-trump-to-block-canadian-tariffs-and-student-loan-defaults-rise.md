## Background & Context
The tariffs on Canada were proposed by **President Trump** as part of his broader trade agenda. The President has argued that the tariffs are necessary to protect American industries and workers, but critics have argued that they will harm the economy and lead to higher prices for consumers. The 6 GOP representatives who voted against the tariffs are:
* **Rep. John Katko** (NY)
* **Rep. Brian Fitzpatrick** (PA)
* **Rep. Will Hurd** (TX)
* **Rep. Chris Smith** (NJ)
* **Rep. Dave Reichert** (WA)
* **Rep. Erik Paulsen** (MN)

These representatives have been joined by Democrats in opposing the tariffs, highlighting the bipartisan nature of the opposition. The vote is a significant defeat for **President Trump**, who has made trade a key part of his agenda.

## Background & Context: Student Loan Defaults
Student loan defaults are a growing concern in the US, with many Americans struggling to pay back their debts. The issue is complex, with many factors contributing to the rise in defaults. Some of the key factors include:
* **Rising tuition costs**: The cost of attending college has increased significantly in recent years, making it harder for students to afford their education.
* **Insufficient financial aid**: Many students are not receiving enough financial aid to cover the cost of their education, leading to a reliance on loans.
* **Poor job prospects**: The job market for recent graduates is highly competitive, making it difficult for students to find well-paying jobs that will allow them to pay back their loans.

The rise in student loan defaults has significant implications for the US economy, as it can lead to a decrease in consumer spending and an increase in debt. Policymakers are under pressure to find solutions to the problem, with some proposing reforms to the student loan system and others advocating for increased funding for financial aid.
