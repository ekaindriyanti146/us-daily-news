## Background & Context
Measles is a highly contagious disease that can cause serious health complications, particularly in young children and individuals with weakened immune systems. The disease is spread through the air when an infected person coughs or sneezes, and it can also be spread through contact with contaminated surfaces. **The measles vaccine**, which is typically administered in two doses, is highly effective in preventing the spread of the disease. However, in recent years, there has been a growing trend of **vaccine hesitancy**, with some parents choosing not to vaccinate their children due to concerns about the safety and efficacy of vaccines. This trend has contributed to the current outbreak of measles, as areas with low vaccination rates are more susceptible to the spread of the disease.

## Analysis
The current outbreak of measles is a significant public health concern, and it highlights the importance of vaccination in preventing the spread of disease. **Dr. Oz's call to action**, urging Americans to get vaccinated against measles, is a crucial step in addressing this issue. By getting vaccinated, individuals can not only protect themselves from the disease but also help to prevent the spread of measles in their communities. Furthermore, **vaccination is a social responsibility**, as it helps to protect vulnerable members of society, such as young children and individuals with weakened immune systems, who may be more susceptible to the disease.
