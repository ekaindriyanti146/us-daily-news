## **Key Takeaways**
* Congress has reached a $1.2 trillion funding deal for the Department of Homeland Security (DHS), Pentagon, and domestic agencies.
* The deal **averts a partial government shutdown** and provides funding for various government agencies until the end of the fiscal year.
* The agreement includes **$728 billion for defense spending** and **$475 billion for non-defense discretionary spending**.
* The deal also includes **$40 billion for disaster relief** and **$10 billion for border security**.

## **Introduction**
In a last-minute effort to avert a partial government shutdown, Congress has clinched a $1.2 trillion funding deal for the Department of Homeland Security (DHS), Pentagon, and domestic agencies. The agreement, which was reached after weeks of intense negotiations, provides funding for various government agencies until the end of the fiscal year. The deal is a significant development, as it **avoids a potentially disastrous shutdown** that could have had far-reaching consequences for the US economy and national security.

## **The Deep Dive**
The funding deal is a comprehensive package that includes funding for a wide range of government agencies and programs. The **$728 billion for defense spending** includes funding for the Pentagon, as well as for various defense-related programs and initiatives. The **$475 billion for non-defense discretionary spending** includes funding for domestic agencies, such as the Department of Education, the Department of Health and Human Services, and the Department of Housing and Urban Development. The deal also includes **$40 billion for disaster relief**, which will provide critical funding for communities affected by natural disasters, and **$10 billion for border security**, which will support efforts to secure the US-Mexico border.

The funding deal is a significant achievement, as it **avoids a partial government shutdown** that could have had serious consequences for the US economy and national security. A shutdown would have resulted in the furlough of hundreds of thousands of federal employees, as well as the disruption of critical government services and programs. The deal is also a testament to the ability of Congress to come together and reach a compromise, despite the intense partisan divisions that have characterized the current legislative session.
