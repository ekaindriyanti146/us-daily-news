## Introduction
A **measles outbreak** has been confirmed at a Texas detention facility, prompting **ICE** to halt all movement at the facility. The outbreak has raised concerns about the health and safety of detainees, particularly children and vulnerable individuals. According to reports, **two cases** of measles have been confirmed at the facility, which held a **5-year-old** and their father. The facility, located in **Dilley, Texas**, is one of the largest immigration detention centers in the United States.

## Background & Context
The **Dilley family detention facility** has been the subject of controversy in recent years, with concerns raised about the treatment and living conditions of detainees. The facility is designed to hold families and children, and has been criticized for its **lack of adequate medical care** and **unsanitary conditions**. The **measles outbreak** has highlighted the need for improved health and safety protocols at the facility, particularly given the **highly contagious** nature of the disease.

### Measles: A Highly Contagious Disease
**Measles** is a highly contagious and potentially life-threatening disease that can spread quickly in crowded and unsanitary conditions. The disease is characterized by **fever, cough, and rash**, and can lead to serious complications, including **pneumonia** and **encephalitis**. **Measles** is particularly dangerous for **young children** and **individuals with weakened immune systems**, who are more susceptible to serious complications.
