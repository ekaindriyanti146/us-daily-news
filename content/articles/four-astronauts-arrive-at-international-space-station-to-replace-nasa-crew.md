* The new crew will conduct scientific research and maintenance tasks during their stay

## Introduction
On a historic day for space exploration, four astronauts from **NASA**'s Crew-12 have arrived at the International Space Station (ISS). The crew, which includes astronauts from the United States, Japan, and Europe, will replace the previous astronauts and join Expedition 74. The SpaceX Crew Dragon spacecraft, which carried the astronauts to the ISS, successfully docked at the station, marking a significant milestone in the mission. The new crew will spend several months on the ISS, conducting scientific research, performing maintenance tasks, and contributing to the ongoing development of the station.

## Background & Context
The ISS is a habitable artificial satellite in low Earth orbit, where astronauts and cosmonauts live and work for extended periods. The station is a joint project between **NASA**, Russia's **Roscosmos**, Japan's **JAXA**, Canada's **CSA**, and the European Space Agency (**ESA**). The ISS serves as a research laboratory, observatory, and testbed for spaceflight technologies. The station is also a crucial platform for space agencies to develop and test the skills and technologies needed for future deep space missions, such as those to the **Moon** and **Mars**.

The Crew-12 mission is the latest in a series of rotations of astronauts to and from the ISS. The crew will conduct a wide range of scientific experiments, including research on the effects of microgravity on living organisms, the behavior of materials in space, and the Earth's climate and weather patterns. The astronauts will also perform maintenance tasks, such as upgrading the station's life support systems and replacing faulty equipment.

### The Journey to the ISS
The Crew-12 astronauts launched from **Kennedy Space Center** in Florida on a SpaceX Falcon 9 rocket, aboard the Crew Dragon spacecraft. The journey to the ISS took approximately 24 hours, during which time the astronauts had to endure intense G-forces, extreme temperatures, and the harsh conditions of space. The Crew Dragon spacecraft is equipped with state-of-the-art technology, including advanced navigation systems, life support systems, and communication equipment.
