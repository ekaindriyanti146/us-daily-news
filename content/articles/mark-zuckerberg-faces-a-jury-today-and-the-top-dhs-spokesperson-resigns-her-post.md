## Introduction
Today, **Mark Zuckerberg**, the CEO of **Meta**, is facing a jury in a landmark social media addiction trial. The trial, which is being closely watched by the tech industry and beyond, could have significant implications for **Big Tech** companies and their responsibilities towards user addiction. As **Zuckerberg** takes the stand, he will be testifying about the company's practices and policies regarding user age and addiction. This comes as some users have reportedly lied about their age when signing up for **Instagram**, highlighting the challenges faced by social media companies in ensuring user safety and well-being.

## Background & Context
The trial is part of a broader conversation about the impact of social media on society, with many experts and lawmakers calling for greater regulation and accountability from **Big Tech** companies. **Meta**, in particular, has faced intense scrutiny over its handling of user data and its role in perpetuating addiction and other negative behaviors. As **Zuckerberg** testifies, he will be facing questions about the company's algorithms, its advertising practices, and its efforts to prevent addiction and promote user well-being.

### The Rise of Social Media Addiction
In recent years, social media addiction has become a growing concern, with many experts warning about the potential links to mental health issues, sleep deprivation, and other problems. **Social media addiction** is characterized by excessive use of social media platforms, often to the point of neglecting other aspects of life, such as work, relationships, and physical health. As **Mark Zuckerberg** and other tech leaders have acknowledged, social media companies have a responsibility to promote healthy usage habits and prevent addiction.
