## Background & Context
For decades, we've been told that **aerobic exercise**, such as running or cycling, is essential for cardiovascular health and weight management. However, new research suggests that this may not be the case. **HIIT**, which involves short bursts of high-intensity exercise followed by brief periods of rest, has been shown to be more effective for improving cardiovascular health and boosting metabolism. Additionally, **strength training** has been found to be crucial for building muscle mass and bone density, reducing the risk of injury and disease. But what about **aging**? As we age, our bodies undergo natural changes that can affect our ability to exercise. However, research has shown that regular physical activity can help to slow **aging** and promote healthy ageing.
