## Background & Context
**Measles** is a highly contagious virus that can cause serious health complications, especially in young children and individuals with weakened immune systems. The virus is spread through the air when an infected person coughs or sneezes, and it can also be transmitted through contact with contaminated surfaces. **Measles** symptoms include fever, cough, runny nose, and a characteristic rash. In severe cases, **measles** can lead to complications such as pneumonia, encephalitis, and even death.

The **measles** exposure at **Philadelphia International Airport** is a significant concern, as it has the potential to spread the virus to a large number of people. The airport is a major hub for domestic and international travel, with thousands of passengers passing through its gates every day. ### Measles Outbreaks in the US
In recent years, there have been several **measles** outbreaks in the United States, primarily due to low vaccination rates in certain communities. Vaccination is the most effective way to prevent the spread of **measles**.
