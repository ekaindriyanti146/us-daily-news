## Background & Context
The life cycle of massive stars is complex and not fully understood. These stars are born in giant molecular clouds and live short, violent lives, ending in a catastrophic explosion known as a **supernova**. However, in some cases, the star may collapse directly into a **black hole**, without the accompanying explosion. This is known as a "failed **supernova**" or "direct collapse." The observation of such an event is extremely rare, as it requires the star to be close enough to Earth to be visible, yet far enough away to be unaffected by the explosion. The **Andromeda galaxy**, being our closest galactic neighbor, provides an ideal location for such observations. The discovery was made possible by the use of advanced telescopes, such as the **Hubble Space Telescope**, and sophisticated observational techniques, including **spectroscopy** and **photometry**.
