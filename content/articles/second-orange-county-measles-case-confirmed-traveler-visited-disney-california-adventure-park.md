## Introduction
A second case of measles has been confirmed in Orange County, California, according to health officials. The infected person, a traveler, visited **Disney California Adventure Park** and may have exposed others to the virus. This comes after a measles alert was issued for **Disneyland**, **LAX**, and **Woodland Hills**. The outbreak has raised concerns about the spread of the virus, particularly among those who are not vaccinated.

The **Orange County Health Care Agency** has confirmed that the second case is linked to the first case, which was reported earlier this week. The agency is working to identify anyone who may have been exposed to the virus and is warning people to be aware of the symptoms of measles.

## Background & Context
Measles is a highly contagious virus that can cause serious illness, particularly among young children and people with weakened immune systems. The virus is spread through the air when an infected person coughs or sneezes, and can also be spread through contact with contaminated surfaces.

In recent years, there has been a resurgence of measles cases in the United States, particularly among unvaccinated communities. The **Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC)** has reported a significant increase in measles cases, with over 1,000 cases reported in 2019.

The **measles vaccine** is highly effective in preventing the spread of the virus, but some people may not be vaccinated due to personal or medical reasons. Health officials are urging people to get vaccinated, particularly if they plan to travel to areas where measles is common.

### Measles Symptoms
The symptoms of measles include:
* Fever
* Cough
* Runny nose
* Red eyes
* Rash

If you or someone you know is experiencing these symptoms, it is essential to seek medical attention immediately.

Vaccination is essential in preventing the spread of measles and other contagious diseases. The **CDC** recommends that all children receive two doses of the measles vaccine, one at 12-15 months and another at 4-6 years.

Adults who are not vaccinated or who are unsure of their vaccination status should also get vaccinated. The **measles vaccine** is available at most healthcare providers and is covered by most insurance plans.
