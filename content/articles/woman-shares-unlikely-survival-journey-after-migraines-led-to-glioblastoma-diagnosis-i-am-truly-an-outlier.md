## Background & Context
**Glioblastoma** is a highly aggressive and malignant form of brain cancer, with a poor survival rate. According to the [American Brain Tumor Association](https://www.abta.org/), the five-year survival rate for patients with **glioblastoma** is approximately 5%. The disease is characterized by the rapid growth of cancer cells in the brain, which can cause a range of symptoms, including headaches, seizures, and changes in personality. In the case of the woman who shared her story, her migraines were initially dismissed as a minor issue, but eventually, she sought medical attention after experiencing persistent and severe headaches. Following a series of tests, including an MRI and biopsy, she was diagnosed with **glioblastoma**. As [CDC](https://www.cdc.gov/) reports, early diagnosis is crucial for effective treatment.
