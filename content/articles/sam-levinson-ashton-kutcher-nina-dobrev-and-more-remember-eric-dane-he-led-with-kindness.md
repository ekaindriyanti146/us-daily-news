According to reports, Dane had been battling **Amyotrophic Lateral Sclerosis (ALS)**, a progressive neurodegenerative disease. ## Background & Context
Eric Dane was born on November 9, 1972, in San Francisco, California. He began his acting career in the late 1990s, appearing in small roles on television shows and in films. His breakthrough role came in 2005 when he was cast as Dr. Mark Sloan, also known as **McSteamy**, in the hit medical drama Grey's Anatomy. Dane's performance earned him a nomination for a **Screen Actors Guild Award** and made him a household name. He went on to appear in other notable roles, including the HBO series Euphoria, where he played the complex and troubled character of Cal Jacobs.

### Early Life and Career
Dane's early life and career were marked by a passion for acting and a determination to succeed. He attended **San Mateo High School** and later studied acting at **Marymount Manhattan College** in New York City. After college, he moved to Los Angeles to pursue a career in acting, landing small roles on shows like **Saved by the Bell** and **Married... with Children**.
