## Introduction
A recent incident in New Mexico has raised concerns about the safety of **raw milk** after a newborn baby died from a **listeria infection**. According to state officials, the mother had consumed **raw milk** while pregnant, which is believed to have caused the infection. This incident has prompted experts to warn against drinking **raw milk** due to the risk of **listeria** and other bacterial infections. In this article, we will delve into the background and context of **raw milk** consumption, analyze the risks associated with it, and discuss the expert opinions on the matter.

## Background & Context
**Raw milk** is milk that has not been pasteurized, which means it has not been heated to a high temperature to kill off bacteria and other microorganisms. While some people believe that **raw milk** is more nutritious and has health benefits, the **CDC** and other health organizations have consistently warned against its consumption due to the risk of bacterial infections. **Listeria**, in particular, is a type of bacteria that can be found in **raw milk** and can cause serious illness, especially in pregnant women, older adults, and people with weakened immune systems.

The **FDA** has also announced new testing of **baby formula** to ensure safety, which is a significant step in protecting the health of infants. However, the incident in New Mexico highlights the need for greater awareness and education about the risks associated with **raw milk** consumption. As [**CDC**](/articles/senior-cdc-official-loss-of-measles-elimination-status-in-u-s-would-be-cost-of-doing-business) officials have stated, "Raw milk can contain harmful bacteria like **E. coli**, **Salmonella**, and **Listeria**, which can be particularly dangerous for pregnant women, older adults, and people with weakened immune systems."

## Analysis
The risks associated with **raw milk** consumption are significant, and experts warn that the benefits do not outweigh the risks.
