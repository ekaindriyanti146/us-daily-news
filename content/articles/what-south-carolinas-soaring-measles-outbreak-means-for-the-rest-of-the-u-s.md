## Background & Context
Measles is a highly contagious viral disease that can cause serious illness and even death. It is spread through the air when an infected person coughs or sneezes, and can also be spread through contact with contaminated surfaces. The disease was declared eliminated in the US in 2000, thanks to a comprehensive vaccination program. However, in recent years, there has been a resurgence of measles cases in the US, largely due to declining vaccination rates in some communities.

The outbreak in South Carolina is particularly concerning, as it has spread rapidly and has affected a large number of people. The state health department has reported that the majority of cases are among unvaccinated individuals, highlighting the importance of vaccination in preventing the spread of the disease.

### The Role of Vaccination
Vaccination is the most effective way to prevent the spread of measles. The measles, mumps, and rubella (MMR) vaccine is highly effective in preventing the disease, and is recommended for all children at 12-15 months of age, with a second dose at 4-6 years of age. However, some parents have opted out of vaccinating their children, citing concerns about the safety and efficacy of the vaccine. This has led to a decline in vaccination rates in some communities, making them more susceptible to outbreaks.
