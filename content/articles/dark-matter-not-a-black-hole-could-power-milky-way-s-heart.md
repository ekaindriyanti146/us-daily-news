## Background & Context
The **Milky Way** galaxy is a **barred spiral galaxy**, consisting of hundreds of billions of stars, gas, and dust. At its center, a **supermassive black hole** was thought to reside, with a mass of approximately four million times that of our sun. This **black hole** was believed to be responsible for the galaxy's observed behavior, including its rotation curve and central density. However, the new study suggests that this **black hole** may not be the primary driver of the **Milky Way**'s behavior. Instead, a compact object composed of **fermionic dark matter** is proposed to be at the galaxy's center. **Fermionic dark matter** is a type of **dark matter** that is made up of particles called **fermions**, which are the building blocks of matter.

### What is Dark Matter?
**Dark matter** is a mysterious and invisible form of matter that makes up approximately 27% of the universe's mass-energy density. It is called "dark" because it does not emit, absorb, or reflect any electromagnetic radiation, making it invisible to our telescopes. Despite its elusive nature, **dark matter**'s presence can be inferred through its gravitational effects on visible matter. **Dark matter** is thought to play a crucial role in the formation and evolution of galaxies, including our own **Milky Way**.
