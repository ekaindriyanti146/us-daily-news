## Background & Context
The **Artemis II** mission is part of NASA's **Artemis program**, which aims to establish a sustainable human presence on the Moon by 2025. The program is a crucial step towards further human exploration of the solar system, with the Moon serving as a stepping stone for missions to **Mars** and beyond. The Artemis II mission will send a crew of four astronauts on a lunar flyby, testing the spacecraft's systems and paving the way for future missions. The crew will include the first woman and the first person of color to visit the Moon, marking a significant milestone in the history of space exploration.

The **Artemis II** spacecraft is a **Space Launch System (SLS)** rocket, which is the most powerful rocket ever built. The rocket is designed to carry heavy payloads to the Moon and beyond, and is capable of reaching speeds of up to 25,000 miles per hour. The spacecraft is equipped with state-of-the-art technology, including advanced life support systems, navigation, and communication equipment.
