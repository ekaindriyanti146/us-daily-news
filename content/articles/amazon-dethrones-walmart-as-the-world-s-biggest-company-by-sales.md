## Introduction
In a significant shift in the retail landscape, **Amazon** has overtaken **Walmart** as the world's largest company by sales. This milestone marks a major turning point in the industry, with **e-commerce** playing an increasingly important role. According to recent reports, **Amazon**'s sales have surpassed those of **Walmart**, with the online retailer generating over $500 billion in revenue in the past year. This achievement is a testament to **Amazon**'s diverse range of products and services, including **cloud computing**, **advertising**, and **grocery delivery**. But what does this mean for **Walmart**, and how will the retail giant respond to this new challenge?

## Background & Context
The retail industry has undergone significant changes in recent years, with the rise of **e-commerce** and **digital transformation**. **Amazon** has been at the forefront of this trend, with its online platform and diverse range of products and services. **Walmart**, on the other hand, has traditionally been a **brick-and-mortar** retailer, with a strong presence in physical stores. However, in recent years, **Walmart** has invested heavily in its **e-commerce** platform, with a focus on **digital transformation** and **artificial intelligence**. Despite these efforts, **Walmart** has struggled to keep pace with **Amazon**'s rapid growth, and the company has now lost its position as the world's largest company by sales.

### Historical Context
The competition between **Amazon** and **Walmart** is not new, with both companies vying for dominance in the retail industry for over two decades. However, in recent years, the competition has intensified, with both companies investing heavily in **e-commerce** and **digital transformation**. **Amazon**'s acquisition of **Whole Foods** in 2017 marked a significant turning point in the competition, with the online retailer expanding its presence in the **grocery delivery** market. **Walmart**, on the other hand, has responded with its own **grocery delivery** service, as well as investments in **artificial intelligence** and **digital transformation**.
