## Key Takeaways
* Ubisoft shares plummet 33% after the company announces a reorganization and cancels six games
* The cancelled games include **Prince of Persia** and other unannounced titles
* The reorganization aims to improve the company's financial performance and focus on core franchises
* Ubisoft's vision for its new **Creative Houses** is to create the best games in the world, in every segment they're in
* The company's decision to cancel games and reorganize has sparked a mixed reaction from gamers and investors

## Introduction
In a shocking move, **Ubisoft** has announced a major reorganization and the cancellation of six games, including the highly anticipated **Prince of Persia** remake. The news has sent the company's shares plummeting 33%, with investors and gamers alike expressing concern about the future of the beloved game developer. As the gaming industry continues to evolve, **Ubisoft** is looking to improve its financial performance and focus on its core franchises. But what does this mean for the company and its fans?

## Background & Context
**Ubisoft** has been a major player in the gaming industry for decades, with a portfolio of iconic franchises including **Assassin's Creed**, **Far Cry**, and **Just Dance**. However, in recent years, the company has faced increased competition from other game developers and publishers, leading to a decline in sales and revenue. The cancellation of **Prince of Persia** and other games is seen as a strategic move to focus on more profitable titles and reduce costs. According to **Yves Guillemot**, **Ubisoft**'s CEO, the company wants to create games that are "the best in the world, in every segment we're in".

## Analysis
The reorganization and game cancellations are a significant blow to **Ubisoft**'s fans, who have been eagerly awaiting the release of **Prince of Persia** and other titles. However, from a business perspective, the move makes sense. By focusing on core franchises and reducing costs, **Ubisoft** can improve its financial performance and increase its competitiveness in the market. The company's decision to cancel games and reorganize is also seen as a necessary step to ensure its long-term survival and success. As **Ubisoft**'s **Creative Houses** begin to take shape, the company is poised to create some of the most innovative and engaging games on the market.

## Quotes & Reactions
"We want them to be the best in the world, in every segment we're in," said **Yves Guillemot**, **Ubisoft**'s CEO, in a statement. "We're committed to creating games that exceed our players' expectations and push the boundaries of what's possible." The news has sparked a mixed reaction from gamers and investors, with some expressing disappointment and others seeing the move as a necessary step for the company's future success. "I'm sad to see **Prince of Persia** cancelled, but I understand the decision," said one gamer. "I just hope **Ubisoft** can deliver on its promise to create amazing games in the future."

## Outlook
As **Ubisoft** moves forward with its reorganization and focuses on its core franchises, the company is poised to create some of the most innovative and engaging games on the market. While the cancellation of **Prince of Persia** and other games is a disappointment to fans, it's a necessary step to ensure the company's long-term survival and success. With its **Creative Houses** and commitment to excellence, **Ubisoft** is ready to take on the challenges of the gaming industry and deliver amazing experiences to its players. As the company looks to the future, one thing is clear: **Ubisoft** is dedicated to creating the best games in the world, and it's willing to make tough decisions to get there.
