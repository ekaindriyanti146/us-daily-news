## Introduction
The Department of Homeland Security (DHS) has partially shut down due to a funding impasse between **Democrats** and the **Trump** administration. In this article, we will examine the impact of the shutdown on different components and employees of the DHS.

## Background & Context
The DHS shutdown is a result of a funding dispute between **Democrats** and the **Trump** administration. The administration has requested additional funding for border security, including the construction of a wall along the US-Mexico border. **Democrats** have opposed the request, citing concerns about the effectiveness and cost of the wall. The impasse has resulted in a partial shutdown of the DHS, with essential employees continuing to work without pay.

The DHS is responsible for a wide range of functions, including border security, cybersecurity, and disaster response. The department employs over 240,000 people, including border patrol agents, TSA screeners, and cybersecurity experts. The shutdown may impact the processing of immigration applications and the issuance of travel documents, as well as the department's ability to respond to natural disasters and cyber threats.
