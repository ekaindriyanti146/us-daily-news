## Introduction
The Sundance Film Festival, one of the most prestigious independent film festivals in the world, has begun its final edition in Park City, Utah. The festival, which has been held in Park City for over 40 years, will feature a diverse range of films, including documentaries, dramas, and comedies. The opening night of the festival was attended by several high-profile celebrities, including **Chris Pine**, **Jenny Slate**, and **Judd Apatow**. The event marked the beginning of a bittersweet celebration, as the festival prepares to move to a new location in future years.

## Background & Context
The Sundance Film Festival was founded in 1978 by **Robert Redford** and has since become a major platform for independent filmmakers to showcase their work. The festival has launched the careers of many notable directors, including **Quentin Tarantino** and **Steven Soderbergh**, and has premiered some of the most iconic films of the past few decades, including **"Sex, Lies, and Videotape"** and **"Napoleon Dynamite"**. Park City has been the home of the festival since its inception, and the city has become synonymous with independent cinema. However, the festival's organizers have announced that this will be the final edition of the festival in Park City, citing the need for a new and more sustainable location.
