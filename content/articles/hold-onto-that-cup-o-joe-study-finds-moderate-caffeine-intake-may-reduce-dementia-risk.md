## Background & Context
Dementia is a complex and multifaceted condition that affects memory, thinking, and behavior. It is a leading cause of disability and death among older adults, and its prevalence is expected to increase significantly in the coming years. While there is no cure for dementia, research has shown that lifestyle factors such as diet, exercise, and social engagement may help reduce the risk of developing the condition. Caffeine, which is a stimulant found in coffee, tea, and other beverages, has been shown to have a range of effects on the brain and body. Moderate caffeine intake has been linked to improved cognitive function, including attention, memory, and processing speed.
