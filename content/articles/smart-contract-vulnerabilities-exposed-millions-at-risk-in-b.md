## Background and Context
Smart contracts are self-executing contracts with the terms of the agreement written directly into lines of code. They are a crucial component of the blockchain ecosystem, enabling the creation of decentralized applications (dApps) and facilitating peer-to-peer transactions without the need for intermediaries. However, the increasing complexity of smart contracts has also introduced new risks and vulnerabilities, which can be exploited by malicious actors. The recent discovery of smart contract vulnerabilities is not an isolated incident - it is part of a larger trend of security risks and challenges facing the blockchain industry.
