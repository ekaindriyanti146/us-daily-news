## Introduction
The ongoing partial government shutdown has entered its third week, with no end in sight. The main point of contention is funding for the Department of Homeland Security (DHS), with Democrats and Republicans unable to reach a deal. On Wednesday, the White House rejected the latest Democratic counterproposal on DHS funding, calling it 'very unserious'. The proposal, which includes reforms to Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) and a path to citizenship for certain immigrants, has been met with skepticism by Republicans. As the shutdown continues, Congress is under pressure to reach a deal, but negotiations are stalled.

## Background & Context
The partial government shutdown began on October 1, after Congress failed to pass a spending bill that included funding for DHS. The shutdown has affected thousands of government employees, who have been furloughed or forced to work without pay. The shutdown has also had a significant impact on the economy, with estimates suggesting that it could cost the US economy billions of dollars. The main point of contention is funding for DHS, with Democrats and Republicans unable to agree on a deal. Democrats have proposed a number of alternatives, including a path to citizenship for certain immigrants and reforms to ICE. However, these proposals have been met with skepticism by Republicans, who are pushing for increased border security measures.

### History of the Shutdown
The partial government shutdown is the latest in a series of shutdowns that have occurred in recent years. In 2018, the government shut down for three days over a dispute about immigration reform.
