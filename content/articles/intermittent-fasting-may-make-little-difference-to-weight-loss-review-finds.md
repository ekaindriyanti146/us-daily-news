### What is Intermittent Fasting?
Intermittent fasting is a dieting method that involves alternating periods of eating and fasting. There are several different methods of intermittent fasting, but most involve restricting calorie intake for certain periods of time, such as 16 hours or 24 hours. During the fasting period, individuals may drink water, black coffee, or other low-calorie beverages, but they are not allowed to eat any food. The idea behind intermittent fasting is that by restricting calorie intake, the body will be forced to burn stored fat for energy, leading to weight loss.

The researchers found that, on average, individuals who practiced intermittent fasting lost a similar amount of weight as those who followed traditional dieting methods. The review also found that intermittent fasting may not be as effective for weight loss as previously thought, and that it may be no better than doing nothing at all.
