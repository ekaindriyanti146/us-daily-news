## Introduction
In a significant move, **United Parcel Service (UPS)** has announced plans to cut up to 30,000 jobs in 2026. This decision comes as the company seeks to reduce its reliance on **Amazon** shipments and optimize its network. The job cuts are expected to affect various aspects of the company's operations, including delivery, logistics, and administration. As the company navigates this transition, it is essential to examine the reasons behind this move and its potential impact on the industry.

## Background & Context
UPS has long been a major player in the logistics industry, with a significant portion of its business coming from **Amazon** shipments. However, in recent years, the company has sought to diversify its customer base and reduce its reliance on the e-commerce giant. This move is part of a broader effort to improve efficiency, reduce costs, and increase profitability. The company has been investing in new technologies, such as automation and data analytics, to streamline its operations and better compete in a rapidly changing market.
