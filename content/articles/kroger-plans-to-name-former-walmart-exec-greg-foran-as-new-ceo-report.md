## Introduction
In a surprise move, **Kroger**, one of the largest grocery retailers in the United States, has announced plans to appoint former **Walmart** executive **Greg Foran** as its new CEO. The news, which was first reported by the **Cincinnati Enquirer** and later confirmed by other sources, including **The Wall Street Journal** and **ABC News**, has sent shockwaves through the retail industry. As **Kroger**'s new CEO, Foran will be responsible for leading the company's strategy and operations, and is expected to bring significant changes to the company's approach to e-commerce, customer service, and competitiveness.

## Background & Context
**Kroger**, which operates over 2,700 stores across the United States, has been facing increasing competition from online retailers such as **Amazon** and **Walmart**, as well as from discount stores like **Aldi** and **Lidl**. In recent years, the company has invested heavily in its e-commerce capabilities, including the launch of a new online shopping platform and the expansion of its curbside pickup service. However, despite these efforts, **Kroger**'s sales have continued to decline, and the company has struggled to keep pace with its competitors.

The appointment of **Greg Foran** as CEO is seen as a major coup for **Kroger**, given his experience and track record at **Walmart**. Foran, who served as the president and CEO of **Walmart**'s U.S. operations from 2014 to 2019, is credited with helping to turn around the company's struggling U.S. business, and is known for his expertise in e-commerce, supply chain management, and customer service.

The appointment of **Greg Foran** as **Kroger**'s new CEO is significant for several reasons.
