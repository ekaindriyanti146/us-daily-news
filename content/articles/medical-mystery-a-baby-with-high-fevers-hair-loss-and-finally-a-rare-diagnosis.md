## **Background & Context**
**Kartagener syndrome** is a rare genetic disorder that affects approximately 1 in 32,000 people worldwide. It's a type of **Primary Ciliary Dyskinesia (PCD)**, which means that the cilia in the body are either absent or don't function properly. Cilia are tiny, hair-like structures that play a crucial role in various bodily functions, including respiratory, auditory, and reproductive systems. In people with **Kartagener syndrome**, the cilia are either immobile or have abnormal movement, leading to a range of symptoms, including chronic respiratory infections, hearing loss, and even infertility.

The baby's symptoms were initially misdiagnosed, with doctors believing that the high fevers and hair loss were caused by a viral infection. However, as the symptoms persisted, further testing was conducted, including genetic testing, which revealed the rare diagnosis. The baby's parents were shocked and devastated by the news, but they're grateful that the condition was finally diagnosed, and their child can receive the necessary treatment.
