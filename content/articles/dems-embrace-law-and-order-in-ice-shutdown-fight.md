## Background & Context
The ICE shutdown fight is a culmination of months of tension between Democrats and Republicans over **immigration policy**. The **Trump administration** has pushed for increased funding for **ICE**, citing the need to enhance national security and enforce immigration laws. Democrats, on the other hand, have argued that the agency's tactics are overly aggressive and that funding should be conditional on reforms. The shutdown fight has been marked by intense negotiation and debate, with both parties dug in on their respective positions. The Democratic Party's shift towards a "law and order" approach marks a significant departure from their previous position, which emphasized the need for **immigration reform** and **protections for undocumented immigrants**.

### Historical Context
The ICE shutdown fight is not an isolated incident, but rather part of a broader pattern of tension between Democrats and Republicans over immigration policy. The **Obama administration**'s efforts to implement **Deferred Action for Childhood Arrivals (DACA)** and **Deferred Action for Parents of Americans (DAPA)** were met with resistance from Republicans, who argued that the measures were unconstitutional. The **Trump administration**'s subsequent efforts to rescind DACA and implement a **travel ban** targeting predominantly Muslim countries have been met with fierce opposition from Democrats. The ICE shutdown fight is the latest iteration of this ongoing debate, with the Democratic Party's shift towards a "law and order" approach marking a significant turning point in the negotiations.
