## The Details
The term 'artificial intelligence' refers to the development of computer systems that can perform tasks that would typically require human intelligence, such as visual perception, speech recognition, and decision-making. When it comes to capitalizing this term, the general rule of thumb is to treat it as a common noun, meaning it should not be capitalized unless it appears at the beginning of a sentence or is part of a title. However, some argue that the term 'artificial intelligence' has become a proper noun in its own right, warranting capitalization due to its significance and widespread use.

## Background and Context
The capitalization of artificial intelligence is not just a matter of grammatical correctness; it also reflects the evolving nature of the technology and its integration into our daily lives. As AI becomes increasingly ubiquitous, the way we refer to it in language is likely to change. The Associated Press Stylebook, a widely used guide for journalists, recommends treating 'artificial intelligence' as a common noun, while other style guides, such as the Chicago Manual of Style, suggest that it can be capitalized when referring to a specific system or technology.
