## Background & Context
The social media addiction trial is a culmination of years of research and debate about the impact of social media on mental health and wellbeing. Studies have shown that excessive social media use can lead to addiction, depression, anxiety, and other negative effects. The case alleges that social media companies have knowingly designed their platforms to be addictive, using techniques such as infinite scrolling, notifications, and algorithms that prioritize engagement over user wellbeing. The plaintiffs claim that **Meta** and **YouTube** have failed to take adequate steps to protect users from the harmful effects of social media addiction.

### The Allegations
The allegations against **Meta** and **YouTube** are significant and far-reaching. The plaintiffs claim that the companies have:
* Designed their platforms to be addictive, using techniques such as infinite scrolling and notifications
* Failed to provide adequate warnings about the risks of social media addiction
* Failed to take adequate steps to protect users from the harmful effects of social media addiction
* Prioritized profits over user wellbeing
