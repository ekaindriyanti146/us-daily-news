## Introduction
Colon cancer, also known as colorectal cancer, is a type of cancer that affects the large intestine. It is one of the most common types of cancer in the US, and its incidence has been increasing in recent years, particularly among young adults. According to recent studies, colon cancer is now the deadliest type of cancer for certain groups of Americans, with scientists sounding the alarm on the alarming rise of colon cancer in people under 50. But what is causing this trend, and what can be done to prevent it?

## Background & Context
Colon cancer is typically associated with older adults, with the majority of cases occurring in people over the age of 50. However, in recent years, there has been a significant increase in the number of colon cancer cases in young adults. According to the [American Cancer Society](https://www.cancer.org/), the incidence of colon cancer in people under 50 has increased by 22% since 2000. This trend is not limited to the US, with similar increases in colon cancer cases observed in other developed countries.

The exact causes of the rise in colon cancer cases in young adults are not yet fully understood, but several factors are thought to be contributing to the trend. A sedentary lifestyle and poor diet are thought to be major contributing factors, with many young adults leading busy lives and relying on processed and convenience foods. Other factors, such as obesity, smoking, and a family history of colon cancer, may also play a role.
