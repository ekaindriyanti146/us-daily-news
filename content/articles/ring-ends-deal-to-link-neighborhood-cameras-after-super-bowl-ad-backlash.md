## Key Takeaways
* **Ring** has ended its deal to link neighborhood cameras with **Flock Safety** after facing backlash over a **Super Bowl** ad.
* The partnership aimed to integrate **Flock**'s community request feature with **Ring**'s neighborhood cameras.
* The decision comes amid concerns over **surveillance** and **privacy**.

## Introduction
In a surprising move, **Ring**, the popular smart doorbell camera company, has canceled its partnership with **Flock Safety**, a community-focused security platform. The decision comes after the company faced significant backlash over a **Super Bowl** ad that promoted the integration of **Flock**'s community request feature with **Ring**'s neighborhood cameras. The partnership, which was announced earlier this year, aimed to enhance community safety by allowing users to request footage from nearby **Ring** cameras. However, the move sparked concerns over **surveillance** and **privacy**, leading to a public outcry and ultimately, the cancellation of the deal.

## Background & Context
The partnership between **Ring** and **Flock Safety** was seen as a way to leverage the power of community-driven security. **Flock**'s platform allows users to request footage from nearby cameras, which can be useful in investigating crimes or identifying suspicious activity. **Ring**, with its vast network of neighborhood cameras, was seen as a natural partner for **Flock**. However, the integration of the two platforms raised concerns over **surveillance** and **privacy**. Critics argued that the partnership would create a vast network of surveillance cameras, potentially infringing on individuals' right to privacy. The backlash was swift, with many taking to social media to express their concerns.

### The Role of the Super Bowl Ad
The **Super Bowl** ad, which promoted the partnership between **Ring** and **Flock**, was seen as a major misstep by the company. The ad, which featured a montage of **Ring** cameras and **Flock**'s community request feature, was criticized for its tone-deaf approach to **surveillance** and **privacy**. Many viewers felt that the ad glossed over the potential risks and consequences of such a partnership, instead focusing on the benefits of community-driven security. The backlash was immediate, with many calling for **Ring** to cancel the partnership.
