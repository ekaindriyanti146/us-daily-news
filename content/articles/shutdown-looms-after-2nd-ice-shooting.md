## Key Takeaways
* A second shooting at an ICE facility has increased the likelihood of a US government shutdown.
* Democrats have vowed not to fund ICE after the shooting, imperiling a spending deal.
* Senate Republicans are unwilling to remove DHS funding, further increasing the chances of a shutdown.
* Senator Angus King has stated that he will not vote for a package with ICE funding, but believes a shutdown can be avoided.
* Senator Schumer has called on the GOP to move a funding package without DHS money to avoid a shutdown.

## Introduction
The United States government is facing a looming shutdown after a second shooting at an Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE) facility. The incident has sparked a heated debate over funding for the agency, with Democrats vowing not to provide funds and Senate Republicans refusing to remove DHS funding from a spending package. As the deadline for a shutdown approaches, lawmakers are scrambling to find a solution to avoid a potentially devastating disruption to government services.

## Background & Context
The second shooting at an ICE facility has brought the issue of funding for the agency to the forefront. Democrats have long been critical of ICE's policies and practices, and the recent incident has only strengthened their resolve to withhold funding. On the other hand, Senate Republicans are adamant that ICE funding is essential for national security and are unwilling to remove it from a spending package. The stalemate has increased the likelihood of a government shutdown, which could have far-reaching consequences for the country.

The issue of ICE funding is complex and multifaceted. Proponents of funding argue that the agency plays a critical role in enforcing immigration laws and protecting national security. However, opponents argue that ICE's policies and practices are often inhumane and ineffective, and that the agency's funding should be redirected towards more humane and effective solutions.

## Analysis
The potential shutdown has significant implications for US citizens. A shutdown would result in the furlough of hundreds of thousands of government employees, disrupting essential services such as law enforcement, healthcare, and transportation. Additionally, a shutdown would have a ripple effect on the economy, potentially leading to widespread job losses and economic instability.

The debate over ICE funding is also highly polarized, with both sides dug in and unwilling to compromise. This polarization has made it difficult for lawmakers to find common ground and pass a spending package. As the deadline for a shutdown approaches, it remains to be seen whether lawmakers can put aside their differences and find a solution to avoid a shutdown.
