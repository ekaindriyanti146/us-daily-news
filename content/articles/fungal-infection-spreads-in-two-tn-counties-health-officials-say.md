## Introduction
A fungal infection outbreak has been reported in two counties in Tennessee, according to health officials. The outbreak has resulted in dozens of cases, with at least one death confirmed. ## Background & Context
Histoplasmosis is a fungal infection caused by the **Histoplasma capsulatum** fungus, which is commonly found in soil and bird droppings. The infection can be spread through the air when contaminated soil or droppings are disturbed, and people inhale the spores. The symptoms of histoplasmosis can range from mild to severe and can include fever, cough, and chest pain. In severe cases, the infection can cause pneumonia, meningitis, and even death.

The outbreak in Tennessee has been reported in two counties, with the majority of cases occurring in **Spring Hill** and **Williamson County**. Health officials have confirmed that the infection is potentially deadly and has raised concerns about the risk across the state. The **Tennessee Department of Health** has launched an investigation to determine the source of the outbreak and to identify any potential links between the cases.
