## Background & Context
The **Artemis 2** mission is a crewed mission that will send astronauts to the lunar surface for the first time in over 50 years. The mission will be crewed by four astronauts, who will spend several days in lunar orbit before returning to Earth. The mission is a significant step towards establishing a sustainable human presence on the lunar surface, and it will pave the way for future missions to Mars and beyond. The **Space Launch System (SLS)** rocket, which will be used for the mission, is the most powerful rocket ever built, and it will provide the necessary thrust to send the astronauts to the moon.

The fueling test is a critical component of the mission, as it will help NASA refine its procedures and ensure the safety of the crew and the spacecraft. The test will involve fueling the rocket with liquid hydrogen and liquid oxygen, which are the propellants used by the SLS rocket. The test will also help NASA identify any issues with the fueling process and make any necessary adjustments before the actual launch.
