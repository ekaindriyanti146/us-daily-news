## Background & Context
Measles is a highly contagious viral disease that can cause serious complications, particularly in young children and individuals with weakened immune systems. The **Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC)** recommends that children receive two doses of the measles, mumps, and rubella (MMR) vaccine to protect against the disease. However, in recent years, there has been a decline in vaccination rates in some areas, contributing to an increased risk of outbreaks.

The current outbreak in Maricopa County is not an isolated incident.
