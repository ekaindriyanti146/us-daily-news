## Background & Context
Black holes are among the most mysterious and fascinating objects in the universe. They are formed when a massive star collapses under its own gravity, causing a massive amount of matter to be compressed into an incredibly small space. This compression creates an intense gravitational field, which warps the fabric of spacetime around the black hole. The point of no return, called the event horizon, marks the boundary beyond which anything that enters cannot escape.

The formation of black holes is still not fully understood, and scientists have been studying the process for decades. The observation of a black hole's birth is a rare event, and it provides a unique opportunity to study the process in detail.
