## Background & Context
The Artemis program was launched in 2019, with the goal of establishing a sustainable human presence on the lunar surface. The program is named after the Greek goddess of the hunt, Artemis, who is also the twin sister of Apollo. The Artemis 2 mission is the second crewed mission in the program, following the successful uncrewed Artemis 1 mission in 2022. The spacecraft will be crewed by four astronauts, who have undergone rigorous training to prepare for the mission. The astronauts have entered quarantine ahead of the launch, to minimize the risk of illness and ensure the success of the mission.

The Artemis 2 mission will involve a lunar flyby, where the spacecraft will orbit the Moon and conduct a series of scientific experiments. The mission will also test the spacecraft's systems and procedures, in preparation for future lunar landing missions. The spacecraft will be launched on a **Space Launch System (SLS)** rocket, which is the most powerful rocket ever built. The SLS rocket will provide the necessary thrust to propel the spacecraft to the Moon and back.
