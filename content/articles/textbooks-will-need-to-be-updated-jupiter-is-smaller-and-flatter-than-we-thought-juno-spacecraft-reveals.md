The **Juno spacecraft** is equipped with a range of advanced instruments, including a magnetometer, a radiometer, and a spectrometer, which have enabled scientists to make precise measurements of **Jupiter's** size and shape.

Additionally, the **Juno spacecraft** has found that **Jupiter** is flatter at the poles and bulging at the equator, with a more pronounced oblateness than previously thought.
