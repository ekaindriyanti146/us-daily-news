## Background & Context
The **Artemis program** is a NASA mission aimed at sending the first woman and the next man to the moon by 2025. The program involves the use of a powerful rocket, the **Space Launch System (SLS)**, and a spacecraft, the **Orion**. The SLS rocket is designed to carry the Orion spacecraft and its crew to the moon and beyond. However, the rocket has been experiencing fuel leaks, which have delayed the program's progress.

The fuel leaks were first detected in 2020, and since then, NASA has been working to fix the issue. The agency has conducted several tests and has made modifications to the rocket's design, but the fuel leaks continue to occur. The most recent incident occurred during a **wet dress rehearsal** test, which is a critical test that simulates the launch countdown.

### Causes of the Fuel Leaks
The exact cause of the fuel leaks is still unknown, but NASA engineers believe that it may be related to the rocket's **hydrogen fuel system**. The system is complex and involves the use of multiple valves, pipes, and tanks. The fuel leaks may be occurring due to a variety of factors, including **design flaws**, **manufacturing defects**, or **operational errors**.
