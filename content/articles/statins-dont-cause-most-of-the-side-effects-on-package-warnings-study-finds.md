## Background & Context
**Statins** have been widely prescribed for decades, and are considered to be one of the most effective ways to lower **cholesterol** levels and reduce the risk of **heart disease**. However, the medication has also been associated with a range of side effects, including **muscle pain**, **diarrhea**, and **fatigue**. These side effects have been listed on the labels of **statin** medications, and have been a source of concern for many patients. However, the new study suggests that many of these side effects are not actually caused by the **statin** medication itself, but rather by other factors, such as the **nocebo effect**. The **nocebo effect** refers to the phenomenon where patients experience side effects because they believe they will, rather than because of any actual effect of the medication.
