The mission is a significant step forward in space exploration, and its success will pave the way for future manned missions to the **Moon**, **Mars**, and beyond.

## Background & Context
The SpaceX Crew-12 mission is the latest in a series of manned missions to the ISS, which have been carried out by NASA and its international partners since 2000. The ISS is a habitable artificial satellite in low Earth orbit, where astronauts and cosmonauts can live and work for extended periods. The station is a vital platform for scientific research, allowing scientists to conduct experiments in microgravity, which cannot be replicated on Earth. The ISS is also a testbed for the development of new technologies and equipment, which will be used on future manned missions to the Moon and Mars.

The SpaceX Crew-12 mission is a significant milestone in the development of private spaceflight, marking the first time that a private company has launched a manned mission to the ISS. **SpaceX**, founded by **Elon Musk**, has been working closely with NASA to develop the Crew Dragon spacecraft, which will carry the astronauts to the ISS. The spacecraft is designed to be reusable, and will be able to carry out multiple missions to the ISS and other destinations in low Earth orbit.
