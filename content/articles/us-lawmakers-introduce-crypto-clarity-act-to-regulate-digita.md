# Breaking News: US Lawmakers Move to Bring Clarity to Crypto Regulation
Who: A bipartisan group of US lawmakers, led by Rep. Tom Emmer (R-MN) and Rep. Darren Soto (D-FL), introduced the Crypto Clarity Act on Wednesday. 
What: The Crypto Clarity Act aims to provide regulatory clarity for digital assets in the United States. 
When: The bill was introduced in the US House of Representatives on Wednesday. 
Where: The bill is set to be debated in the US House of Representatives in the coming weeks. 
Why: The lawmakers hope to provide a clear and comprehensive framework for the regulation of digital assets, which have been a subject of controversy in recent years. 
How: The bill proposes to achieve this by amending the Securities Act of 1933 and the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 to exclude digital assets from the definition of a security.

## The Details
The Crypto Clarity Act is a significant development in the ongoing debate over the regulation of digital assets in the United States. The bill proposes to amend the Securities Act of 1933 and the Securities Exchange Act of 1934 to exclude digital assets from the definition of a security. This would provide clarity for investors, businesses, and regulators, and would help to promote the growth of the digital asset industry.
