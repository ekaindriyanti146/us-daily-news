## Introduction
The **National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA)** has announced that it will be moving up the critical fueling test for the **Artemis 2** moon rocket to January 31. This test is a crucial step in the preparation for the launch of the **Artemis 2** mission, which is scheduled to take place later this year. The **Artemis 2** mission is a significant milestone in **NASA**'s plans to return humans to the lunar surface by 2025. ## Background & Context
The **Artemis 2** mission is the first crewed mission of the **Artemis program**, which aims to return humans to the lunar surface by 2025. The mission will send four astronauts on a lunar flyby, testing the **Space Launch System (SLS)** rocket and the **Orion** spacecraft. The **Artemis 2** mission is a crucial step towards establishing a sustainable presence on the lunar surface, with the ultimate goal of sending humans to Mars and beyond.

The fueling test is a critical component of the **Artemis 2** mission preparation. The test will involve fueling the **SLS** rocket with liquid hydrogen and liquid oxygen, which will power the rocket's engines. The test will also involve testing the rocket's propulsion systems, as well as the **Orion** spacecraft's systems.
