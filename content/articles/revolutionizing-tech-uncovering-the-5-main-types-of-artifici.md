## The Details: Understanding the 5 Types
The five main types of artificial intelligence are categorized based on their functionality and complexity: Reactive Machines, Limited Memory, Theory of Mind, Self-Aware, and Artificial General Intelligence (AGI). 
1. **Reactive Machines** are the simplest form of AI, designed to react to currently existing situations. They don't have the ability to form memories or use past experiences to influence decisions. An example of reactive machines is the Deep Blue chess player, which defeated a world chess champion but couldn't learn or improve over time from its experiences.
2. **Limited Memory** AI systems can learn from data to make decisions but have limited memory. They use this data to improve their performance over time. Most chatbots and virtual assistants fall into this category, as they can learn from interactions but their memory is limited to the data they've been trained on.
3. **Theory of Mind** AI refers to machines that understand emotions, beliefs, and intents of other entities (like humans). This level of AI is still largely theoretical and is being researched for applications in social robotics and customer service.
4. **Self-Aware** AI involves systems that have a consciousness of their own existence and can reflect on their internal state. This is the most advanced form of AI and, as of now, exists more in theory than in practical applications.
5. **Artificial General Intelligence (AGI)** is the holy grail of AI research, where machines possess the ability to understand, learn, and apply knowledge across a wide range of tasks, similar to human intelligence. AGI is still a topic of ongoing research and debate.
