## Background & Context
Black holes are regions of spacetime where gravity is so strong that nothing, not even light, can escape. They are formed when a massive star collapses in on itself, causing a massive amount of matter to be compressed into an incredibly small space. Supermassive black holes, like the one observed in this study, are found at the centers of galaxies and can have masses millions or even billions of times that of the sun. These black holes play a crucial role in the evolution of galaxies, regulating the growth of stars and the formation of planetary systems.

### The Physics of Black Hole Outbursts
The outburst observed in this study is known as a "tidal disruption event" (TDE). TDEs occur when a star wanders too close to a black hole and is torn apart by the strong gravitational forces.
