## Background & Context
The American fashion industry has a rich history, with iconic brands like **Ralph Lauren** and **Calvin Klein** dominating the market for decades. However, in recent years, the industry has faced significant challenges, including declining sales, increased competition from fast-fashion brands, and growing concerns about sustainability. In response, a new generation of designers and brands has emerged, focusing on innovative materials, digital platforms, and sustainable production methods. This shift is also driven by changing consumer behavior, with online sales expected to account for over 30% of the fashion market by 2025. As **Olivia Wilde** noted, "The fashion industry is at a crossroads, and it's exciting to see a new generation of designers and brands emerging with a focus on sustainability and technology."
