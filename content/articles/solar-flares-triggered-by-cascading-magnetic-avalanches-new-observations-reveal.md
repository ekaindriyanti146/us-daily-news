## Background & Context
The sun's corona, its outer atmosphere, is a complex and dynamic region, filled with magnetic fields, plasma, and energy. The corona is where solar flares occur, and understanding the mechanisms that drive these events is crucial for predicting and mitigating their effects on Earth. Researchers have long suspected that magnetic reconnection, a process in which magnetic fields interact and release energy, plays a key role in triggering solar flares. However, the exact mechanisms behind this process were not well understood.

The new study, which utilized data from spacecraft and ground-based telescopes, provides the most detailed views yet of the magnetic avalanches that trigger solar flares. By analyzing the data, the researchers found that the magnetic avalanches occur when a buildup of magnetic energy in the sun's corona is suddenly released, causing a cascade of events that ultimately lead to a solar flare.
