## Introduction
In a move to enhance the user experience of **Windows 11**, **Microsoft** has announced the introduction of a built-in network speed test. This feature, which is currently available in the **Release Preview Channel**, will allow users to easily check their internet speed without the need for third-party software. The network speed test is a welcome addition to **Windows 11**, as it will provide users with a convenient way to troubleshoot internet connectivity issues and optimize their network settings. In this article, we will delve into the details of the built-in network speed test, its benefits, and what it means for **Windows 11** users.

## Background & Context
The introduction of the built-in network speed test is part of **Microsoft**'s ongoing efforts to improve the overall user experience of **Windows 11**. In recent months, **Microsoft** has released several updates that have added new features and improved the performance of the operating system. The built-in network speed test is a significant addition, as it will provide users with a easy-to-use tool to check their internet speed and troubleshoot connectivity issues. According to **Microsoft**, the network speed test will be available in the **Settings** app, and will allow users to check their upload and download speeds, as well as their ping time.

### How it Works
The built-in network speed test uses a combination of **Microsoft**'s own servers and third-party services to provide accurate and reliable results. When a user runs the speed test, their device will connect to a nearby server, which will then measure the user's upload and download speeds, as well as their ping time. The results will be displayed in an easy-to-read format, allowing users to quickly identify any issues with their internet connection. In addition to providing a simple and convenient way to check internet speed, the built-in network speed test will also provide users with detailed information about their network configuration, including their IP address, subnet mask, and default gateway.
