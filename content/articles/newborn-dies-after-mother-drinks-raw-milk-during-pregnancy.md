## Background & Context
Raw milk, also known as unpasteurized milk, is milk that has not been heat-treated to kill off bacteria and other microorganisms. While some proponents of raw milk argue that it is more nutritious and flavorful than pasteurized milk, the risks associated with its consumption far outweigh any potential benefits. Raw milk can contain a range of harmful bacteria, including E. coli, Salmonella, and Listeria, which can cause severe illness and even death. Pregnant women are particularly vulnerable to these infections, as their immune systems are suppressed during pregnancy. The CDC recommends that pregnant women only consume pasteurized milk and milk products, as the risk of infection from raw milk can be severe.

### Raw Milk and Pregnancy
The risks associated with raw milk consumption during pregnancy are well-documented. A study published in the Journal of Food Protection found that pregnant women who consumed raw milk were at a higher risk of developing listeriosis, a severe infection caused by Listeria bacteria. Another study published in the American Journal of Epidemiology found that raw milk consumption during pregnancy was associated with an increased risk of miscarriage and stillbirth. These findings highlight the importance of avoiding raw milk and raw milk products during pregnancy, and instead opting for pasteurized alternatives.
