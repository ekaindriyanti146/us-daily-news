## Key Takeaways
* European leaders are warning of an urgent need for self-defense due to concerns over the reliability of the United States.
* The warnings come amid rising tensions between the US and Europe, with some European leaders questioning the US commitment to their security.
* The issue was highlighted at the recent [Munich Security Conference](/articles/munich-security-conference), where European leaders discussed the need for greater self-reliance in defense matters.
* The US has traditionally been a key player in European security, but some European leaders are now seeking to reduce their dependence on the US and develop their own defense capabilities.
* The development of European self-defense capabilities could have significant implications for the future of transatlantic relations and the global balance of power.

## Introduction
The relationship between the United States and Europe has been a cornerstone of international relations for decades, with the US providing a security umbrella for European nations. However, in recent years, there have been growing concerns over the reliability of the US as a security partner. European leaders have been warning of an urgent need for self-defense, citing concerns over the US commitment to their security and the rising threat of global instability. This shift in European thinking has significant implications for the future of transatlantic relations and the global balance of power. As the [European Union](/articles/european-union) seeks to develop its own defense capabilities, the question on everyone's mind is: what does this mean for the future of European security?

## Background & Context
The warnings from European leaders are not new, but they have taken on a greater sense of urgency in recent years. The [Trump administration](/articles/trump-administration) has been criticized for its unpredictable approach to foreign policy, which has led to concerns over the US commitment to European security. The [US withdrawal from the Iran nuclear deal](/articles/us-withdrawal-from-iran-nuclear-deal) and the [US-China trade war](/articles/us-china-trade-war) have also contributed to a sense of uncertainty among European leaders. Furthermore, the [Russian annexation of Crimea](/articles/russian-annexation-of-crimea) and the ongoing conflict in [Ukraine](/articles/ukraine-conflict) have highlighted the need for European nations to take a more active role in their own defense.

The [European Union](/articles/european-union) has been seeking to develop its own defense capabilities, including the creation of a [European Defense Fund](/articles/european-defense-fund) and the development of a [European Army](/articles/european-army). However, these efforts have been met with skepticism by some, who argue that they are unnecessary and could undermine the [NATO alliance](/articles/nato-alliance). Despite these concerns, European leaders remain committed to developing their own defense capabilities, citing the need for greater self-reliance in the face of rising global instability.

## Analysis
The development of European self-defense capabilities has significant implications for the future of transatlantic relations and the global balance of power.
