## Introduction
Autism, also known as Autism Spectrum Disorder (ASD), is a complex neurodevelopmental disorder that affects millions of people worldwide. For decades, scientists have been trying to understand the causes of autism, and recent discoveries have sparked hope that it might be preventable in certain cases. ## Background & Context
Autism is a multifaceted disorder that affects communication, social interaction, and behavior. The exact causes of autism are still not fully understood, but research suggests that it is a combination of **genetic** and **environmental factors**. Studies have shown that **prenatal exposure** to certain toxins, **maternal infection** during pregnancy, and **advanced parental age** can increase the risk of autism. Additionally, **genetic mutations** and **chromosomal abnormalities** can also contribute to the development of autism. To better understand the complexities of autism, it is essential to explore the [**latest research on genetic predisposition**](/articles/latest-research-on-genetic-predisposition) and [**environmental factors**](/articles/environmental-factors-in-autism).

## Analysis
The possibility of autism prevention has significant implications for **US citizens**. Autism affects approximately 1 in 54 children in the United States, and the economic burden of autism is estimated to be over $200 billion annually. If autism can be prevented in certain cases, it could lead to significant cost savings and improved quality of life for individuals and families affected by the disorder. Furthermore, **early intervention** and **prenatal care** can make a significant difference in reducing the risk of autism. As [**Kamala Harris**](/articles/kamala-in-la-la-land) once stated, "Early intervention is key to improving outcomes for children with autism."
