## Introduction
The **National Aeronautics and Space Administration (NASA)** has started the countdown for the **Artemis 2** Space Launch System (SLS) wet dress rehearsal, a critical fueling test for the mission. The test is a crucial step in preparing for the **Artemis 2** mission, which aims to send astronauts to the **Moon**. The mission is part of **NASA**'s larger plan to return humans to the **Moon** and eventually send them to **Mars**. The **Artemis 2** mission will be crewed by four astronauts, who will travel farther from Earth than anyone before them.

## Background & Context
The **Artemis 2** mission is the second mission in **NASA**'s **Artemis** program, which aims to return humans to the **Moon** by 2025. The mission will test the **Space Launch System (SLS)** rocket and the **Orion** spacecraft, which will be used for future lunar and Mars missions. The **SLS** rocket is the most powerful rocket ever built, with the capability to carry heavy payloads to deep space. The **Orion** spacecraft is designed to carry astronauts to the **Moon** and beyond, providing a safe and reliable way for humans to explore space.

The **Artemis 2** mission will be a crewed mission, with four astronauts on board. The astronauts will travel to the **Moon** and back, testing the **SLS** rocket and the **Orion** spacecraft. The mission will also test the **European Service Module (ESM)**, which will provide propulsion, power, and life support systems for the **Orion** spacecraft.
