Measles is a highly contagious disease that can have serious consequences, particularly for young children and individuals with weakened immune systems. The **measles vaccine**, which is typically administered in two doses, is highly effective in preventing the disease. However, in recent years, **vaccine hesitancy** has become a major concern, with some parents opting not to vaccinate their children due to misinformation or concerns about vaccine safety. This has led to a decline in vaccination rates, creating an environment in which diseases like measles can spread quickly.

The current outbreak is not limited to a single region, with cases reported in several states, including **Texas**, **California**, and **New York**.
