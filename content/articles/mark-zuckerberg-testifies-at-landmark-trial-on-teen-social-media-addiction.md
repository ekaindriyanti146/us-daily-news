In a highly anticipated and closely watched trial, **Mark Zuckerberg**, the CEO of **Meta**, took the stand to testify on the issue of teen social-media addiction. The trial is part of a broader conversation about the impact of social media on society, particularly on young people. In recent years, there has been growing concern about the effects of social media on mental health, self-esteem, and addiction. **Meta**, as one of the largest and most influential social media companies, has faced increasing scrutiny over its role in shaping the online experiences of its users. The company has faced criticism for its algorithms, which prioritize engagement and advertising revenue over user well-being. As a result, **Meta** has faced calls for greater regulation and accountability.

In response to these concerns, **Mark Zuckerberg** has emphasized the company's commitment to improving the well-being of its users, particularly teenagers. In his testimony, Zuckerberg highlighted the company's efforts to introduce new features and tools aimed at reducing the negative impacts of social media on young people. These include measures such as time limits, parental controls, and resources for users struggling with addiction or mental health issues.
