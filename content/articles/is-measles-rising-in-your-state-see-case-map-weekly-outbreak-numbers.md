## Introduction
Measles, a highly contagious and potentially serious disease, is on the rise in the US. According to recent reports, several states are experiencing outbreaks, with the number of cases increasing significantly over the past few years. As a result, health officials are sounding the alarm, urging Americans to get vaccinated and take precautions to prevent the spread of the disease. In this article, we will delve into the current state of measles outbreaks in the US, exploring the causes, consequences, and measures being taken to combat the disease.

## Background & Context
Measles was once considered eliminated in the US, thanks to widespread vaccination efforts. However, in recent years, the disease has made a comeback, with outbreaks reported in several states. The CDC has attributed the rise in measles cases to a combination of factors, including **low vaccination rates** in certain communities and the ease of international travel, which allows the disease to spread quickly across borders. Additionally, misinformation about the safety and effectiveness of vaccines has led to a decline in vaccination rates in some areas, further contributing to the spread of the disease.

## Analysis
The rise of measles outbreaks in the US is a concerning trend, particularly for young children and people with weakened immune systems, who are most vulnerable to the disease. Measles can have serious complications, including **pneumonia**, **encephalitis**, and **death**. Furthermore, the disease can have significant economic and social impacts, including missed work and school days, as well as strain on the healthcare system. As such, it is essential for Americans to take the necessary precautions to prevent the spread of the disease, including getting vaccinated and practicing good hygiene.
