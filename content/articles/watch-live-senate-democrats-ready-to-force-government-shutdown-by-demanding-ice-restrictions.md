* The move could lead to a government shutdown if an agreement is not reached

* The Trump administration has vowed to oppose any attempts to restrict ICE's authority

## Introduction
As the deadline for a funding bill approaches, Senate Democrats are preparing to force a government shutdown by demanding restrictions on Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE). The move is part of a larger effort by Democrats to push back against the Trump administration's immigration policies, which they argue are overly aggressive and inhumane. With the clock ticking down to a potential shutdown, lawmakers are scrambling to reach an agreement, but the prospects for a deal remain uncertain.

The Democrats' push for ICE restrictions is centered on a proposal to limit the agency's ability to detain and deport undocumented immigrants. The plan would require ICE to prioritize the detention of immigrants who pose a threat to public safety, rather than targeting those who have committed minor offenses or are seeking asylum. The move is seen as a key test of the Democrats' ability to influence immigration policy, and could have significant implications for the millions of undocumented immigrants living in the United States.

## Background & Context
The debate over ICE restrictions is just the latest chapter in a long-running battle between Democrats and Republicans over immigration policy. The Trump administration has made cracking down on undocumented immigration a central plank of its agenda, and has implemented a series of policies aimed at increasing deportations and tightening border security. Democrats, on the other hand, have pushed for a more nuanced approach, emphasizing the need for a pathway to citizenship for undocumented immigrants and increased funding for border security measures.

The current funding bill, which must be passed by the end of the week to avoid a government shutdown, has become a focal point for the immigration debate. Democrats are seeking to include language in the bill that would restrict ICE's authority to detain and deport undocumented immigrants, while Republicans are pushing to increase funding for the agency and expand its powers. The standoff has raised the specter of a government shutdown, which could have significant impacts on federal services and the economy.

### History of ICE
ICE was created in 2003, as part of a broader reorganization of the federal government's homeland security apparatus. The agency is responsible for enforcing federal immigration laws, including the detention and deportation of undocumented immigrants. Over the years, ICE has become a lightning rod for controversy, with critics arguing that the agency has prioritized deportations over humanitarian concerns and has failed to adequately protect the rights of immigrants.

On the other hand, limiting ICE's authority could lead to increased public safety, as the agency is forced to prioritize the detention of immigrants who pose a threat to public safety.
