## Introduction
The Venezuelan parliament has passed a new amnesty law, paving the way for the release of opposition politician **Leopoldo Lopez**, who has been detained since 2014. The move is seen as a significant step towards reconciliation and stability in the country, which has been plagued by political unrest and economic crisis in recent years. The law, which was passed with the support of the ruling **PSUV** party, aims to provide a fresh start for those who have been detained for their political views. However, the move has also sparked skepticism among some opposition leaders, who argue that it is an attempt by the government to silence critics and consolidate power.
