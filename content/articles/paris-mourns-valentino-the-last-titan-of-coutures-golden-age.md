## Introduction
Valentino Garavani, the iconic Italian fashion designer, has passed away at the age of 93. Born on May 11, 1932, in Voghera, Italy, Valentino rose to fame in the 1960s with his exquisite and elegant designs. He was known for his stunning haute couture creations, which were worn by some of the most famous women in the world, including **Jacqueline Kennedy Onassis** and **Elizabeth Taylor**. With a career spanning over six decades, Valentino left an indelible mark on the fashion industry, and his passing is being mourned by designers, models, and fashion enthusiasts around the world.

## Background & Context
Valentino's journey in fashion began at a young age, when he started working as an apprentice to his aunt, a fashion designer in Milan. He later moved to Paris, where he worked with the renowned designer **Guy Laroche**. In 1959, Valentino opened his own fashion house in Rome, which quickly gained popularity for its stunning and sophisticated designs. Throughout his career, Valentino was known for his attention to detail, his use of luxurious fabrics, and his ability to create clothes that made women feel like royalty. His designs were not only beautiful but also timeless, and he was often referred to as the "Last Emperor" of high fashion.

### Rise to Fame
Valentino's big break came in the 1960s, when he designed a collection of clothes for **Jacqueline Kennedy Onassis**. The First Lady of the United States was so impressed with Valentino's designs that she asked him to create a wardrobe for her, which included a stunning white gown that she wore to a gala dinner at the White House. This exposure helped to establish Valentino as a major player in the fashion industry, and his designs soon became sought after by celebrities and royalty from around the world.

### Legacy
Valentino's legacy is immeasurable. He was a true pioneer in the fashion industry, and his designs continue to inspire and influence designers today. His fashion house, which is now run by his business partner, **Pierpaolo Piccioli**, continues to produce stunning and sophisticated clothes that are worn by women around the world. Valentino's impact on the fashion industry extends beyond his designs, however. He was a true artist and a master of his craft, and his passion and dedication to fashion have inspired countless young designers to follow in his footsteps.
